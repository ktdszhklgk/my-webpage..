Ultracentrifuge

An ultracentrifuge is a powerful laboratory instrument used to separate components of a mixture based on their density. It works by spinning samples at extremely high speeds, generating strong centrifugal forces that can separate particles, such as proteins, nucleic acids, and cells, from a solution.

Key features include:

  • High Speed: It can reach speeds of up to 100,000 revolutions per minute (RPM) or more, leading to accelerations that can exceed 1,000,000 times gravity.
  • Rotors: Different types of rotors (fixed-angle, swinging bucket, etc.) can be used depending on the sample and separation needs.
  • Applications: Commonly used in biochemistry, molecular biology, and clinical laboratories for tasks like purifying proteins, isolating organelles, and separating lipids.
  • Safety: Due to the high speeds and potential hazards from sample materials, ultracentrifuges are designed with safety features to contain any failures.
